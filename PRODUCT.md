# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

People opening a link from Twitter/X on their phone, in a "this is funny, show me" mood. They want a laugh in under 30 seconds and a screenshot-worthy card to share. Secondary: friends they send it to. Zero patience for onboarding; the joke must land on the first screen.

## Product Purpose

ALIBI generates realistic excuses grounded in the user's real location and live weather ("plausible deniability as a service"). Success = the user copies an excuse or shares the certificate PNG. The result card IS the product; everything else is the form you fill to get it.

## Brand Personality

Deadpan, bureaucratic, absurd. The joke is played completely straight: a fictional government agency ("Bureau of Plausible Deniability") that notarizes your lies with real meteorological evidence. The interface never winks — the copy does. Three words: official, deadpan, unhinged.

## Anti-references

- Meme-generator neon/brutalist chaos (Impact font, rainbow gradients) — the parody dies if the design is already joking.
- Generic SaaS landing page (hero, feature grid, gradient CTA) — this is a single-purpose toy, not a startup.
- Sketchy hand-drawn "quirky" illustration — amateurish, off-register. The bureau is *professional*.

## Design Principles

1. **Play it straight.** Every visual choice imitates real bureaucratic artifacts: government forms, rubber stamps, typewritten certificates, dot-matrix gauges. Humor comes from the collision of formality and content.
2. **The certificate is the product.** One strong visual. The form exists to feed it; the share PNG is its portable twin.
3. **Fast to the punchline.** One screen, three inputs, one button. No explanation needed.
4. **Evidence everywhere.** Real weather data, timestamps, document numbers, barcodes — fake precision is the brand's comedy engine.
5. **Mobile is the stage.** Opened from a tweet, on a phone, in portrait. Desktop is the desk the document sits on.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA. Body text ≥4.5:1 on paper. All controls keyboard-operable with visible focus. `aria-live` for generation status and results. Full `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives (stamp slam → fade, needle sweep → instant). Geolocation is optional — manual city search is a first-class path, not a fallback modal.
